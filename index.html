About Me

I'm a Control and Automation Engineering student at Yıldız Technical University, Istanbul. My work sits at the intersection of robotics, control theory, and artificial intelligence.

I've had the opportunity to work as a Research Intern at Eindhoven University of Technology (TU/e), where I developed simulation platforms for Multi-Robot Task Allocation strategies. I'm passionate about building systems where multiple autonomous agents coordinate to solve complex real-world problems.

When I'm not coding or tuning controllers, I enjoy exploring new research papers on swarm robotics and reinforcement learning.

Projects

Multi-UAV Reconnaissance & Task Allocation System

Built a 10-drone (4 scout + 6 worker) autonomous reconnaissance pipeline in ROS 2, PX4 SITL, and Gazebo. Features OpenCV-based target detection, pinhole-model geo-localization, distance-based sensor fusion, and a greedy nearest-first MRTA strategy for payload delivery.

ROS 2 PX4 Gazebo Python OpenCV

Comparative Analysis of Supervised MRTA Strategies

Designed and implemented a modular simulation framework to benchmark centralized vs. decentralized MRTA strategies, performing a comprehensive empirical analysis of Greedy, Hungarian, and Market-Based algorithms under different supervisory controls.

Python PyGame NumPy MRTA

Flight Controller Design for Quadcopter UAVs

Led a capstone team to design and benchmark PID (with Active Disturbance Rejection), LQR (with Luenberger State Observer), and Robust Backstepping controllers for a 6-DOF quadcopter, achieving stable flight under nonlinear dynamics and external disturbances.

MATLAB Simulink Control Theory LQR PID
